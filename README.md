The Smart Energy Optimization System for Universities is a desktop-based application designed to monitor, analyze, and optimize electricity consumption across university departments. The system helps institutions reduce energy waste, lower electricity bills, measure carbon emissions, and evaluate the potential benefits of renewable energy adoption.

This project is particularly relevant for universities in developing countries like Pakistan, where energy costs are rising and efficient resource management is becoming essential.

üéØ Problem Statement

Many universities face the following challenges:

High electricity bills

Energy waste in empty classrooms and offices

No centralized monitoring of department-wise energy usage

Lack of carbon emission awareness

No structured evaluation of renewable energy options

Currently, most institutions rely on manual observation rather than data-driven decision-making for energy management.

üí° Proposed Solution

We propose a Desktop-Based Energy Monitoring and Optimization System that:

Tracks department-wise electricity consumption

Calculates monthly electricity cost

Measures carbon footprint

Simulates solar panel installation benefits

Provides sustainability scores and recommendations

Generates graphical reports for management

The system acts as a decision-support tool for university administration to improve sustainability and reduce operational costs.
